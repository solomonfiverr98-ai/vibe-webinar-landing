# Copywriting: Voice & Tone Guidelines

## Brand Personality
* Direct and honest — no hype, no false promises
* Relatable — speak like a friend who figured it out
* Urgent — create genuine FOMO without being pushy
* Empathetic — acknowledge their struggles first
* Results-focused — always lead with outcomes

## Grammar & Mechanics
* **Headlines:** Bold, specific, benefit-driven. Numbers when possible.
* **Body copy:** Short sentences. One idea per line. White space is your friend.
* **CTAs:** Specific and action-first. "Reserve My Free Spot" not "Submit"
* **Avoid:** Hype words, vague promises, long paragraphs, corporate language
* **Use:** "You" language, specific numbers, relatable struggles, clear outcomes

## Conversion Psychology
* Pain first — acknowledge struggle before offering solution
* Specificity builds trust — "$1,000" beats "good money"
* Social proof reduces risk — show others succeeded
* Urgency drives action — limited spots, live only
* Simple next step — one clear action at a time

## Copy Examples

**Headline style:**
"How I Made My First $1,000 Online Without Coding" — specific, relatable, curiosity

**Pain point style:**
"You watched videos… but nothing worked.
You joined programs… but got confused.
You started something… but didn't finish."
Short. Punchy. Relatable.

**CTA style:**
"Reserve My Free Spot Now →" — personal, specific, urgent

**Social proof style:**
"This training opened my eyes. I finally understand how to start."
— Daniel O.
Short. Real. Specific emotion.
